Date/Time Action Actor/Details Evidence/Impact
January 2015 Initial access: Spear-phishing emails with malware sent to Bangladesh Bank employees. Lazarus Group (North Korean hackers) At least one employee opened a malicious ZIP file, installing malware.
May 15, 2015 Money laundering preparation: Five fictitious accounts opened at Rizal Commercial Banking Corporation (RCBC) in Manila. Lazarus Group accomplices Accounts dormant with $500 deposits until heist; used fake IDs.
Spring–Summer 2015 Lateral movement: Attackers map network, steal SWIFT credentials. Lazarus Group Undetected due to lack of monitoring; accessed SWIFTLIVE system.
January 29, 2016 Pre-attack escalation: Attackers gain deeper access to SWIFT system. Lazarus Group Positioned for fraudulent transfers; no alerts triggered.
February 4, 2016, 8:36 PM–10:30 PM (Dhaka time) Heist execution: 35 fraudulent SWIFT transfer requests sent to New York Fed for $951 million. Lazarus Group Requests to Philippines and Sri Lanka; exploited weekend timing.
February 4–5, 2016 Transfers processed: Five requests succeed ($101M); $81M to RCBC, $20M to Sri Lanka. Lazarus Group $850M blocked due to typo (“Shalika Fundation”).
February 5, 2016 Malware cover-up: Malware deletes transaction logs, manipulates printer output. Lazarus Group Delayed detection; printer failure initially dismissed as glitch.
February 5–6, 2016 Money laundering: $81M in Philippines converted to pesos via FX broker, funneled to casinos. Lazarus Group accomplices Funds untraceable due to casino exemptions from AML laws.
February 6, 2016 Detection: Printer restarted, revealing 35 transfer requests. Bangladesh Bank staff Panic ensued; contacted New York Fed, SWIFT.
February 8, 2016 Attempted mitigation: Bangladesh Bank requests RCBC to freeze funds. Bangladesh Bank Too late; funds already laundered during Chinese New Year.
February–March 2016 Investigation: FireEye and others confirm North Korean involvement. FireEye, U.S. agencies Linked to Lazarus Group via malware and TTPs.
May 6, 2016 RCBC fallout: President Lorenzo Tan resigns, taking “moral responsibility.” RCBC Internal probe launched; branch manager questioned.
March 2019 Lawsuit: Bangladesh Bank sues RCBC in U.S. court for complicity. Bangladesh Bank Seeks recovery of $81M; RCBC countersues for defamation.
September 2018 U.S. indicts Park Jin Hyok for this and other cyberattacks. U.S. DOJ Confirmed Lazarus Group, North Korean state involvement.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 13 months (January 2015 to February 6, 2016), allowing attackers to infiltrate, map networks, steal credentials, and execute the heist undetected.

Step 1: Initial Access (Jan 2015) Step 2: Lateral Movement (Spring–Summer 2015) Step 3: Collection/Exfiltration (Spring–Summer 2015) Step 4: Malware Deployment (Feb 4–5, 2016) Step 5: Detection (Feb 6, 2016)

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time was like a thief casing a bank vault for over a year, unnoticed due to lax security. Industry reports suggest median dwell times in 2016 were ~150 days; Bangladesh Bank’s 13 months was unusually long, enabling meticulous planning and execution.
