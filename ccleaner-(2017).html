Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 6 months (March 11, 2017–September 13, 2017), allowing attackers to compromise Piriform’s network, prepare malicious builds, and distribute malware undetected.

Step 1: Initial Access (Mar 11, 2017) Step 2: Lateral Movement (Mar–Apr 2017) Step 3: Collection/Exfiltration (Apr–Jul 2017) Step 4: Malware Deployment (Aug 2–Sep 12, 2017) Step 5: Detection (Sep 13, 2017)

What went wrong: No MFA, reused credentials.		 Accessed second computer via RDP, installed backdoor.
Factor: No network segmentation, no IDS.		 Stole admin credentials via keylogger; prepared ShadowPad.
Factor: No DLP or anomaly detection.		 Distributed malicious CCleaner to 2.27M users.
Factor: No build integrity checks.		 Detected by Morphisec and Cisco Talos; C2 shut down.
Total time: Enabled espionage targeting 40 tech firms.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time was like a saboteur tampering with a factory’s production line for months, unnoticed due to lax security. Industry reports suggest median dwell times in 2017 were ~100 days; CCleaner’s 6 months was prolonged, enabling a targeted supply-chain attack.
