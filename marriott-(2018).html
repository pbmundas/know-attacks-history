Attack Timeline

Date/Time Action Actor/Details Evidence/Impact
July 2014 Initial access: Attackers gain unauthorized access to Starwood’s reservation database. Suspected Chinese state-sponsored hackers Likely exploited weak credentials or outdated systems; undetected for years.
November 2015 Starwood breach reported: Separate 2014 credit card breach at 50+ properties; Marriott announces acquisition. Starwood/Marriott 40,000 payment cards affected; acquisition proceeds without full IT audit.
September 2016 Marriott acquires Starwood: Inherits vulnerable IT infrastructure, no integration to Marriott’s systems. Marriott Starwood systems remain unpatched, no MFA or robust monitoring.
September 8, 2018 Detection: Accenture’s security tool (IBM Guardium) flags suspicious database query. Accenture/Marriott Query by admin account not tied to user; Remote Access Trojan (RAT) and MimiKatz found.
November 2018 Investigation: Marriott confirms breach, decrypts stolen data, identifies 383 million records affected. Marriott/Forensic teams 5.25 million unencrypted passport numbers, 8.6 million card details; data exfiltrated since 2014.
November 30, 2018 Disclosure: Marriott announces breach to public, notifies law enforcement. Marriott Stock drops 5%; public trust eroded.
July 2019 ICO penalty notice: Proposes £99 million fine for GDPR violations. UK ICO Cites failure to secure data, inadequate M&A due diligence.
October 30, 2020 Fine reduced: ICO finalizes £18.4 million fine, acknowledging Marriott’s cooperation. UK ICO Reduced from £99 million due to mitigation efforts.
October 9, 2024 Settlement: Marriott agrees to $52 million penalty with 49 U.S. states and FTC. Marriott/FTC/States Requires data minimization, MFA, third-party audits for 20 years.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 4 years (July 2014 to September 2018), from initial access to detection, due to inherited vulnerabilities and lack of monitoring.

Step 1: Initial Access (Jul 2014) Step 2: Collection/Spread (2014–2018) Step 3: Detection/Containment (Sep 8, 2018) Step 4: Recovery (Sep 2018–2024)
Compromised Starwood database via weak credentials.
What went wrong: No MFA, outdated systems.		 Data exfiltrated, re-encrypted to evade detection.
Factor: No SIEM, no file integrity monitoring.		 IBM Guardium flags query; RAT, MimiKatz found.
Factor: External tool detection; no internal IDS.		 Notified customers, settled lawsuits, enhanced security.
Total time: $1B+ in losses, $52M penalties.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time was exceptionally long (4 years vs. 2018 median ~78 days) due to unintegrated legacy systems, no MFA, and inadequate monitoring. Detection relied on an external tool (Accenture’s Guardium), exposing Marriott’s reactive security posture. Rapid containment post-detection mitigated further exfiltration.
