Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was 76-79 days (May 13 to July 29/30, 2017), the period attackers resided undetected. This allowed extensive reconnaissance and exfiltration.

Step 1: Initial Access (May 13) Step 2: Discovery (May-June) Step 3: Privilege Escalation (Ongoing) Step 4: Exfiltration (May-July) Step 5: Detection Trigger (July 29)
Exploit CVE-2017-5638; drop web shells. What went wrong: Unpatched system despite alerts. Scan network, find plaintext credentials in config files. Factor: No segmentation allowed lateral movement. Use creds to access databases. Factor: Legacy systems with weak access controls. Run queries, compress/encrypt data, send via HTTPS. Factor: Expired SSL cert blinded monitoring tools. Cert renewal reveals traffic. Total time enabled ~265 data dumps.

Dwell time is like a spy hiding in your house—longer stays mean more damage. Experts: Median dwell time in 2017 was 101 days (per Mandiant); Equifax's was shorter but still costly due to no EDR/FIM.

Learnings

Root Cause Analysis Impact Assessment Detection Opportunities
Using 5 Whys: Why breached? Unpatched vulnerability. Why unpatched? No inventory of Apache Struts. Why no inventory? Siloed IT/security teams. Why siloed? CSO under legal, not CIO. Why? Organizational misalignment prioritizing growth over security.
Other causes: Plaintext storage of creds, short log retention (14-30 days), 324 expired certs, legacy ACIS complexity.
Beginners: Root causes are the "why" behind failures.
Experts: Fishbone diagram factors: People (human error), Process (patch policy gaps), Technology (legacy systems).		 Data: 148M affected; 209K credit cards, 182K dispute docs.
Financial: $1.4B in costs (2019); $1.38B FTC settlement; stock drop 31%.
Reputational: Trust erosion; CEO/CIO resignations.
Regulatory: Fines, consent orders for audits.
Beginners: Impacts hit wallets and privacy. Experts: BIA metrics: RTO exceeded due to legacy systems.

Missed: March scans could have caught Struts if inventory existed; FIM would detect web shells; longer logs (vs. 14 days) for forensics; network segmentation to block lateral moves.
Opportunities: Renew certs routinely; SIEM rules for query spikes; EDR on endpoints.
Failures: 19-month cert expiration; no redundancy in patch alerts.
Beginners: Detection is like alarms—set them right. Experts: Sigma rule example: detection: logsource: category: database | selection: event: query_count > 1000 in 1h.
