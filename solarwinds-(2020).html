Attack Timeline

Date/Time Action Actor/Details Evidence/Impact
September 2019 Preparation: Attackers compromise SolarWinds’ Orion build environment. Suspected Russian SVR (Cozy Bear/APT29) Injected SUNBURST malware into Orion updates (versions 2019.4–2020.2.1 HF1).
March 2020 Initial access: Trojanized Orion updates deployed to ~18,000 customers. SVR (suspected) Backdoor enabled access to networks of FireEye, Microsoft, U.S. agencies.
March–December 2020 Espionage: Attackers escalate access in select targets, deploy additional malware (TEARDROP, SUNSPOT). SVR (suspected) Targeted U.S. Treasury, Commerce, DHS; stole FireEye red team tools.
December 8, 2020 Detection: FireEye identifies breach in its network, links to SolarWinds Orion. FireEye SUNBURST backdoor identified; FireEye discloses publicly.
December 13, 2020 Disclosure: SolarWinds confirms supply-chain attack, notifies SEC. SolarWinds ~18,000 customers potentially affected; stock drops 17%.
December 15, 2020 Mitigation: FireEye, Microsoft, GoDaddy deploy kill switch for SUNBURST. FireEye/Microsoft/GoDaddy C2 domain repurposed to block malware; Microsoft Defender updated.
January 2021 Attribution: U.S. government attributes attack to Russia. U.S. Intelligence Community Linked to SVR; sanctions imposed on Russia in April 2021.
2021–2022 Recovery: SolarWinds patches Orion, faces lawsuits, enhances security. SolarWinds Costs estimated at $20–90M; class-action lawsuits ongoing.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 15 months (~September 2019 to December 2020), from initial compromise to detection, due to the stealth of SUNBURST and lack of supply-chain monitoring.

Step 1: Initial Access (~Sep 2019) Step 2: Collection/Spread (Mar–Dec 2020) Step 3: Detection/Containment (Dec 8, 2020) Step 4: Recovery (Dec 2020–2022)
SUNBURST injected into Orion build.
What went wrong: No supply-chain code auditing, weak build security.		 Espionage via backdoor, additional malware deployed.
Factor: No IDS for supply-chain traffic, stealthy TTPs.		 FireEye detects anomaly; kill switch deployed.
Factor: External detection, rapid containment.		 Patched Orion, enhanced security, faced lawsuits.
Total time: $20–90M costs, reputation damage.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time (~450 days vs. 2020 median ~95 days) was prolonged due to SUNBURST’s stealth, mimicking legitimate traffic, and lack of supply-chain security. Detection by FireEye and rapid kill switch deployment mitigated further damage, but the breach exposed systemic supply-chain vulnerabilities.
