Attack Timeline

Date/Time Action Actor/Details Evidence/Impact
October/November 2016 Initial access: Hackers compromise an administrator account to access Deloitte’s global email server on Azure. Unknown threat actor(s) Account lacked 2FA; provided unrestricted access to email system.
October 2016–March 2017 Data collection: Hackers access up to 5 million emails, including client data (usernames, passwords, IP addresses, health info). Unknown threat actor(s) Undetected due to lack of monitoring; data from 350 clients potentially exposed.
October 13, 2016 Password reset notice: Deloitte mandates password changes for U.S. employees, possibly indicating breach suspicion. Deloitte Email sent to 244,000 staff; no public disclosure.
March 2017 Detection: Deloitte identifies breach in email platform. Deloitte Internal investigation launched; codenamed “Windham.”
April 27, 2017 Legal response: Deloitte hires Hogan Lovells to review “possible cybersecurity incident.” Deloitte/Hogan Lovells Focused on tracing hacker activity; conducted from Rosslyn, Virginia office.
September 25, 2017 Public disclosure: The Guardian reports breach, citing sources; Deloitte confirms “cyber incident.” Deloitte/The Guardian Deloitte claims only six clients “impacted”; notifies affected clients and regulators.
October 10, 2017 Scope clarification: Sources reveal up to 350 clients’ data in compromised server, including U.S. government entities. The Guardian Contradicts Deloitte’s claim; includes sensitive attachments (security/design details).
Post-September 2017 Ongoing investigation: Deloitte continues to analyze compromised data; no attribution confirmed. Deloitte Unable to identify lone wolf, rival, or state-sponsored actor; no reported disruption.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 5–6 months (October/November 2016–March 2017), allowing hackers to access and potentially exfiltrate sensitive client data undetected.

Step 1: Initial Access (Oct/Nov 2016) Step 2: Collection/Exfiltration (Oct 2016–Mar 2017) Step 3: Detection (Mar 2017) Step 4: Disclosure (Sep 2017)
Compromised admin account via single password.
What went wrong: No 2FA, weak access controls.		 Accessed 5M emails, client data.
Factor: No DLP, no anomaly detection.		 Identified via internal review; Hogan Lovells engaged.
Factor: Delayed detection due to no SIEM.		 Public disclosure after media report; minimized scope.
Total time: Enabled extensive data exposure.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time was like a thief roaming an office for months, unnoticed due to no security alarms. Industry reports suggest median dwell times in 2017 were ~100 days; Deloitte’s 5–6 months was prolonged, enabling significant data exposure due to inadequate monitoring.
