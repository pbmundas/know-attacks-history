Attack Timeline

Date/Time Action Actor/Details Evidence/Impact
Early March 2011 Spearphishing emails sent to RSA employees with malicious Excel attachment. Chinese state-sponsored group (suspected PLA Unit 61398) Emails titled “2011 Recruitment Plan.xls” exploited Adobe Flash zero-day.
March 1-3, 2011 Initial access: Poison Ivy RAT deployed via phishing payload. Attackers Compromised employee workstations; backdoor established.
March 2011 Persistence and escalation: Backdoors and stolen credentials used to access sensitive systems. Attackers Access to SecurID seed data servers.
Mid-March 2011 Data collection and exfiltration: SecurID token seed data stolen via FTP. Attackers Password-protected RAR files exfiltrated; used in later attacks on defense contractors.
March 17, 2011 RSA detects anomalous activity; breach investigation begins. RSA Security Internal monitoring flagged unauthorized access.
March 18, 2011 RSA confirms breach; notifies customers and engages incident response. RSA Public disclosure via open letter; no specific victim data stolen.
April 2011 Remediation: RSA replaces 40 million SecurID tokens for customers. RSA $66 million in costs; reputational damage.
May-June 2011 Follow-on attacks: Stolen SecurID data used against Lockheed Martin and others. Attackers Compromised contractor systems; broader defense sector impact.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 14-20 days (early March to March 17, 2011), the period attackers operated undetected in RSA’s network. This allowed phishing, persistence, escalation, and data exfiltration.

Step 1: Initial Access (Early March) Step 2: Persistence (March) Step 3: Privilege Escalation (March) Step 4: Exfiltration (Mid-March) Step 5: Detection (March 17)
Spearphishing with Adobe Flash zero-day via Excel attachment.
What went wrong: Lack of email filtering and user awareness.		 Deployed Poison Ivy RAT for backdoor access.
Factor: No endpoint detection for RAT behavior.		 Used stolen credentials to access SecurID servers.
Factor: Weak privilege management.		 Exfiltrated seed data via FTP in RAR files.
Factor: No egress filtering for FTP traffic.		 Anomalous access detected via internal monitoring.
Total time: Enabled theft of critical SecurID data.

Dwell Time Insight: The short dwell time was like a quick heist, but sufficient for significant data theft due to targeted phishing. Experts: Median dwell time in 2011 was ~200 days (per Mandiant); RSA’s ~15 days was short but impactful due to sensitive data.

Using 5 Whys: Why breached? Phishing with zero-day. Why successful? No email filtering. Why no filtering? Weak email security policies. Why weak? Underinvestment in threat awareness. Why underinvested? Prioritized product over security.
Other causes: No EDR for RAT detection, poor privilege controls, no FTP egress monitoring.

Experts: Fishbone factors: People (user error), Process (no email filters), Technology (no EDR).		 Data: SecurID seed data stolen; no customer PII reported.
