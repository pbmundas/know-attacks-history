~March 2020 Initial access: Attackers infiltrate FireEye’s network via SUNBURST backdoor in Orion software. SVR (suspected) Access to internal systems; reconnaissance begins undetected.

December 15, 2020 Mitigation: FireEye, Microsoft, GoDaddy deploy kill switch for SUNBURST. FireEye/Microsoft/GoDaddy Command-and-control domain repurposed to block malware; Microsoft Defender updated.
December 2020–2021 Response: FireEye collaborates with FBI, Microsoft; resets passwords, enhances security. FireEye/FBI/Microsoft No evidence of stolen tools used; stock drops 7–8% in after-hours trading.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 9 months (~March to December 8, 2020), from initial access to detection, due to the stealth of SUNBURST and lack of supply-chain monitoring.

Step 1: Initial Access (~Mar 2020) Step 2: Collection/Spread (Mar–Dec 2020) Step 3: Detection/Containment (Dec 8, 2020) Step 4: Recovery (Dec 2020–2021)
SUNBURST backdoor via Orion update.

Total time: Stock drop, no direct fines.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time (~270 days vs. 2020 median ~95 days) was extended due to the sophisticated SUNBURST malware mimicking legitimate traffic and FireEye’s reliance on a trusted third-party (SolarWinds). Internal detection and rapid countermeasure release mitigated further damage, but the breach exposed supply-chain vulnerabilities.
