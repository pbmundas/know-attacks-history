Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was likely several months (exact start unknown, detected November 24, 2014), allowing attackers to map the network, steal data, and deploy malware undetected.

Step 1: Initial Access (Pre-Nov 2014) Step 2: Lateral Movement (Months Prior) Step 3: Collection/Exfiltration (Months Prior) Step 4: Malware Deployment (Nov 24, 2014) Step 5: Detection (Nov 24, 2014)
Spear-phishing compromised credentials.
What went wrong: No 2FA, weak phishing training.		 Navigated network undetected.
Factor: No segmentation or monitoring.		 Exfiltrated 100GB (SSNs, films, emails).
Factor: No data loss prevention (DLP).		 Deployed Shamoon-like wiper malware, erasing systems.
Factor: No real-time malware detection.		 Detected after malware activation and screen takeover.
Total time: Enabled extensive data theft and system destruction.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time was like a thief freely roaming a house for months, unnoticed due to inadequate security. Median dwell time in 2014 was ~205 days (industry reports); Sony’s months-long dwell was typical but catastrophic due to sensitive data exposure and infrastructure wipeout.
