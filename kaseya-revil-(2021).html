Attack Timeline

Date/Time Action Actor/Details Evidence/Impact
Pre-July 2, 2021 Preparation: REvil affiliates exploit zero-day vulnerabilities (CVE-2021-30116) in Kaseya VSA software. REvil (Sodinokibi) ransomware group SQL injection and authentication bypass allow remote code execution; targeted MSPs.
July 2, 2021 Initial infection: Malicious VSA update deployed to ~1,500 MSPs and 60,000 endpoints. REvil affiliates PowerShell script disables Defender, deploys ransomware; encrypts files with .encrypted extension.
July 2, 2021 Detection: Kaseya incident response team identifies security incident, shuts down VSA servers. Kaseya Recommends on-premises VSA shutdown; affects 800–1,500 businesses in 17 countries.
July 3, 2021 REvil claims responsibility: Demands $70M for universal decryptor on dark web forum. REvil Threatens to leak stolen data; largest RaaS ransom demand to date.
July 4, 2021 Kaseya confirms zero-day: Details CVE-2021-30116 (SQL injection, auth bypass) in VSA. Kaseya Advisory released; patches developed; MSPs urged to isolate systems.
July 5–20, 2021 Recovery efforts: Kaseya works with victims; REvil site goes offline (July 13). Kaseya/REvil Site outage suspected due to internal conflict or law enforcement; no decryptor provided.
July 21, 2021 Decryptor obtained: Kaseya secures decryption tool from trusted third party. Kaseya Tool distributed to victims; recovery begins for ~800–1,500 businesses.
July–August 2021 Investigation: FBI, CISA attribute to REvil; lawsuits filed against Kaseya. FBI/CISA Class-action suits for negligence; REvil affiliates arrested in Romania (July 27).
October 2021 Legal fallout: Kaseya faces multiple lawsuits for supply-chain vulnerability. Kaseya Settlements ongoing; highlights RaaS risks.
2022–2023 REvil shutdown: U.S. disrupts REvil infrastructure; affiliates arrested. U.S. DOJ Operation Cronos; reduces RaaS threat but not eliminated.

Dwell Time Analysis

Dwell time was approximately 1 day (July 2, 2021), from initial infection to detection, due to rapid ransomware deployment but swift containment.

Step 1: Initial Access (Pre-Jul 2) Step 2: Malware Deployment (Jul 2) Step 3: Detection/Containment (Jul 2) Step 4: Recovery (Jul 21+)
Zero-day in VSA exploited.
What went wrong: Unpatched software, no supply-chain checks.		 Ransomware encrypts files, demands $70M.
Factor: No EDR, no anomaly detection.		 Kaseya detects incident, shuts down servers.
Factor: Rapid response, no initial SIEM.		 Decryptor distributed; lawsuits filed.
Total time: 800–1,500 businesses disrupted.

Dwell Time Insight: The dwell time (~24 hours vs. 2021 median ~21 days) was ultra-short due to ransomware’s immediate disruption, but preparation (zero-day) took months. No MFA and unpatched VSA enabled access; swift shutdown limited spread.
